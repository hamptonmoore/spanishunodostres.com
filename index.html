  • About Us

    • Spanish Uno Dos Tres is a classroom where children and adults of all ages learn to communicate effectively in Spanish. Classes are dynamic and exciting, moving quickly and emphasizing participation, language skills, and cultural education. Each Uno Dos Tres class is limited to six students who are continually engaged, creating a rapid path toward the ultimate goal of fluency in Spanish.

  • Meet The Teacher

    • Daniela Zeppos

      She was born and raised in La Paz, Bolivia. She studied in a German School from 1st thru 12th grade. In 1999 she relocated to the United States to attend University of Maryland, which then she completed her Bachelors degree in Spanish and German linguistics. Daniela has worked for ten years in a Montessori school, teaching children from the ages of 3 to 12 years old. While at Lone Oak Montessori, she engaged her students to learn Spanish by learning songs, playing games and memorizing phrases. Daniela also encouraged her students and the school community to participate in evening events like “ Feria Latino Americana”. Students and family members gathered to learn about Latin American countries by eating and experiencing student’s country presentations. After her tenure at Montessori, Daniela moved on to teach at Barnesville School where she revamped the Spanish program for third to eight grade and took over the exchange program with Leonardo DaVinci in Lima Peru. While at Barnesville, Daniela traveled with students and parents extensively in South America and Europe. Daniela continued organizing events to celebrate the Latin heritage with events she called at Barnesville, “Noche Latina”. Students and teachers performed skits, dances, songs ending the event with a Latin fair educating the attendees about Latin America. As Daniela moved on with her career, she joined Montgomery County Public School and taught Spanish 1 thru 5. In addition, Daniela has been involved promoting internationality by working with EF High School exchange year. Daniela places international exchange students in the area with local High Schools. Daniela has always envisioned helping young and adult students alike learn languages such as Spanish and German.

  • What We Offer

      What We Offer

      Click on the course description to view the full description

      Elementary
      Elementary Classes (E-E2)

      All E (Elementary) classes will be taught at an Elementary beginner level. Students will be able to learn colors, numbers, verbs, phrases, and songs in a very interactive way. Each course will be a month long and will continuously vary depending on the month. *Short Spanish literature readings will be read to students in order to create fluency and understanding of the Spanish language.

      Middle School/High School Classes
      Beginner 1 Class (B1)

      A B1 Beginner 1 class is considered a “Spanish 1” level student. This course will introduce students to the fundamentals of the Spanish language. Students will not only be able to learn about the language, but they will be exposed to the culture by learning about all 20 different Spanish countries, songs and games. Students will also be encouraged to read beginner books in Spanish to build new vocabulary.

      Beginner 2 Class (B2)

      A B2 Beginner 2 class is considered a “Spanish 2” level student. This course will assume students have previously been exposed to the language before and therefore; reinforcement and review will be done at each session. Students will learn about cultural aspects of all 20 different Spanish countries. Songs and games will also be part of our lessons. At this level students will be asked to read short and compressed Spanish books in order to build new vocabulary.

      Beginner 3 Class (B3)

      A B3 Advanced 1 class is considered a “Spanish 3” level student. This course will encourage students to try their best to hold the entire class in the targeted language. Readings, discussions and grammar will be applied at this level. Students will be given the option to sign up to take the National Spanish examination in order to receive a diploma after several months of attending the course.
