  • About Us

    • Spanish Uno Dos Tres is a classroom where children and adults of all ages learn to communicate effectively in Spanish. Classes are dynamic and exciting, moving quickly and emphasizing participation, language skills, and cultural education. Each Uno Dos Tres class is limited to six students who are continually engaged, creating a rapid path toward the ultimate goal of fluency in Spanish.

  • Meet The Teacher

    • Daniela Zeppos

      Daniela Estenssoro Zeppos is fluent in Spanish, German, and English. Born and raised in La Paz, Bolivia, Daniela attended a German school from grades 1 to 12. She moved to the United States in 1999, where she completed her bachelor’s degree in Spanish and German linguistics at the University of Maryland, College Park.

      Read More

      Daniela taught students from age 3 to 12 at Lone Oak Montessori School in Bethesda for ten years. At Lone Oak, she engaged students as they learned to memorize phrases, sing songs, and play games in Spanish. She encouraged the entire school community to participate in Spanish and Latino programs, including her own Feria Latino Americana (Latin American Fair). Students and their families gathered to eat and experience presentations on each Latin American country. After leaving Lone Oak, Daniela was hired to rethink and reestablish the Spanish program at Barnesville School in Barnesville, Maryland. Daniela’s passion for organizing events to highlight Hispanic and Latino heritage found an outlet in “Noche Latina,” an annual evening dedicated to exploring Latino culture with the entire Barnesville School community, including the wide variety of plays, dances, songs (and yes, of course, food) of Latin America. While at Barnesville, Daniela was also charged with establishing strong leadership of the annual exchange program with Colegio Leonardo da Vinci, a K–12 school in Lima, Peru. Never one to sit still for very long, Daniela also continued to travel extensively during the summer, sponsoring student exchange programs and cultural exchange trips to South America and Europe. More recently, Daniela has taught Spanish levels I to V at the high school level for Montgomery County Public Schools. She has continued to promote cultural exchange and international travel through her work with Cambridge, Mass.–based EF, a world leader in international programs, through which Daniela places international exchange students with high schools and families in the Washington/Maryland region. Now Daniela has founded her own language school, Spanish Uno Dos Tres, for youngsters and adults alike to learn languages as a door to exploring a wider world. She is creative, passionate, and enthusiastic about Spanish — a highly skilled teacher with a vibrant personality and a gift for making others feel welcome and comfortable. Daniela lives in Montgomery County, Maryland, with her husband and their three daughters.

  • What We Offer

      What We Offer

      Click on the course description to view the full description

      Toddler

      Our T (Toddler) classes are taught in a very interactive and dynamic way. Our younger students will be exposed to short phrases and simple vocabulary while interacting with basic Spanish songs. Music and movement will be deeply integrated in this class, as an effective way to bring fun into learning Spanish.

      Elementary Classes (E-E2)

      All E (Elementary) classes will be taught at a level appropriate to students who have not had any Spanish instruction. Students will learn colors, numbers, verbs, phrases, and songs in an interactive setting, and will participate in some literature reading to help develop understanding of the cadence and sounds of Spanish. Each course meets for four weeks.

      Middle School/High School Classes (B1-B3, C)
      Beginner 1 Class (B1)

      Our B1 class is roughly equivalent to Spanish 1 level in school. This course introduces students to the fundamentals of the Spanish language. Students will be exposed to Spanish and Latino cultures through lessons on all 20 Spanish-speaking countries, songs, and games. Students will also read beginner-level books in Spanish to build new vocabulary and familiarity with the language.

      Beginner 2 Class (B2)

      Our B2 class is roughly equivalent to a Spanish 2 level in school. The material assumes that students have previously been exposed to the language, though reinforcement and review are integral parts of each class session. Students will continue learn about many cultural aspects of all 20 different Spanish-speaking countries. Students will be read short or condensed books, songs, and games to build new vocabulary and comfort with Spanish.

      Beginner 3 Class (B3)

      A B3 Advanced 1 class is considered a “Spanish 3” level student. This course will encourage students to try their best to hold the entire class in the targeted language. Readings, discussions and grammar will be applied at this level. Students will be given the option to sign up to take the National Spanish examination in order to receive a diploma after several months of attending the course.

      Immersion (C)

      A Immersion class is designed for native Spanish speakers or students that have already been attending a Spanish immersion program. Classes will be taught ONLY in Spanish, teaching students how to debate and discuss recent world topics. Native Spanish literature and writings will be reinforced in order to strengthen students reading and writing skills.

      Adult Classes (A1, A2)
      Adult Beginner 1 (A2)

      Our A1 Beginner class gives adults the opportunity to learn the basics of Spanish. Many students attending this course may have taken lessons in the past or may be familiar with the language. Basic grammar concepts will be taught in this course, so adults can apply their knowledge at work or in basic conversations. Readings, TV and/or audio programs are encouraged to expose student to the language as effectively as possible.

      Conversational Spanish Class (A2)

      Our A2 Conversational Spanish class for advanced Adult students wanting to practice their Spanish conversation skills. During class we discuss recent topics, movies, books, and students’ experiences. The Immersion class is conducted entirely in Spanish.
